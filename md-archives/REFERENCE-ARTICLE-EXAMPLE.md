# 📰 Reference Example: How I Format Articles

## ✅ Good Article (Like Your 70-Meter Crack Article)

This is the style I follow when you ask me to add articles:

```html
<section style="padding: 20px 0;">
<div class="container">

<p>ROAD INFRASTRUCTURE — A massive 70-meter crack has appeared on Perungudi Road in Chennai, causing panic among local residents who blame recent construction activities for the damage.</p>

<p>According to eyewitness accounts, the crack developed over the past week and has now expanded to approximately 70 meters in length, running across the width of the road near the IT corridor intersection. Residents report heavy vehicles and ongoing construction work in the area as potential causes.</p>

<p><strong>Impact on Commuters</strong></p>

<p>The damaged road section is affecting thousands of daily commuters who use this route to access the Old Mahabalipuram Road (OMR) IT corridor. Traffic officials have temporarily cordoned off the affected area and diverted vehicles through alternative routes, leading to increased congestion during peak hours.</p>

<p><strong>Official Response</strong></p>

<p>Greater Chennai Corporation officials have been notified and are expected to inspect the site by the end of the week. Meanwhile, local residents have submitted a complaint to the civic body, demanding immediate repairs and an investigation into the cause of the structural damage.</p>

<div style="background:#e7f5e7; border-left:4px solid #22c55e; padding:1.5rem; margin:1.5rem 0; border-radius:5px;">
<strong>For Residents:</strong> If you notice similar road issues in your area, report to GCC helpline or visit chennaicorporation.gov.in
</div>

<p><em>This is a developing story. We will update with official statements as they become available.</em></p>

</div>
</section>
```

---

## ✅ What Makes This Good:

1. **Newsworthy headline angle**
2. **Simple, readable paragraphs**
3. **Practical info for residents**
4. **Links to official sources**
5. **Box callout for action**
6. **No complex HTML/tables**
